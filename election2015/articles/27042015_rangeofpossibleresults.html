UK Election 2015 - articles

Apr 27th 2015

What are the extremes of what could happen on May 7th for Labour and the Conservatives?

The current projection has seat totals of Con 271 / Lab 276.

Situation : Highest Conservative lead in a poll in April

The highest Conservative leads in the past month were in todays Ashcroft poll and ICM's poll of April 13th. They both had 6 point leads over Labour with Conservatives on 36% and 38% respectively.

Plugging these two polls into my model give a Conservative seat total of 322, just short of an actual majority. These two polls have extremely low Labour percentages in Scotland and despite it being an overall increase of around 2% on the 2010 election share, they drop by 18 to 239 seats. The Conservative vote percent is almost identical to 2010 but they manage to increase their seat total by gaining from the Lib Dems.

Situation : Highest Labour lead in a poll in April

The highest Labour lead in April was 6% with Panelbase on April 9th. Using this poll in the model give Labour 322 seats to the Conservatives 254. I don't use Panelbase normally in my model due to them not breaking down their vote totals regionally in their tables (that I've managed to find). The Scottish part of the model in particular gets very messed up by this.

After that their highest lead was Survation with 36% and a 4% lead on April 9th. Using this in my model gives Labour 292 seats to the Conservatives 243. A Lab/Lib coalition would be 6 short of a majority with 320.

Situation: +/- 2.5% on Current model averages

In England, Scotland and Wales, Con/Lab are currently on 36.6/32.5, 14.1/27.1 and 24.4/39.5 respectively. The current seat totals are 271 Con / 276 Lab.

Adding 2.5% to Conservative vote percentages and subtracting it from Labour gives 284 Con / 264 Lab.

Doing the opposite for Labour gives 260 Con / 287 Lab.

The only time a 2 party coalition would have a majority in either of these situation is Lab/SNP in the latter with a total of 336.

Conclusion

It seems likely we are heading for a hung parliament. The polls would have to be very wrong or a dramatic change will have to occur in the next 10 days for this not to be the case.

Who will win most seats seems to be close to a toss-up in my model right now. Small variances from current polling averages give either party the lead.
